About Us

VMKS, Dungarpur is a community based voluntary organization was formed in 1997 by group of active leaders to raise issues of Vagad area which comprises Dungarpur and Banswara district of the southern Rajasthan. ”Vagad” word derived from Vagadi language which means "forest". Earlier this region was rich with forest land , but by keeping the laws and policies that came from British Raj till after independence, the entire region and the people especially the tribal communities along with destruction of natural resources were forced to migrate to nearby state- Gujarat & Maharashtra for livelihood. This new situation led to the breakdown of the community system which had been going on for centuries, due to which the natural wealth of this entire area was gradually being taken out the natural rights of the people. In this condition, the only support for their livelihood was migrating from seasonal agriculture to becoming laborers. They were forced to exploitation, injustice and where the value of their hard work determined by the market system. Dungarpur district of Vagad region being a tribal dominated area, a schedule tribal population constitutes about 65 percentage of the total as per the census of the India 2001. Government made and implemented many schemes for tribal development but there was no change in the lives of the tribal's community. In such a situation, the active leadership of the Vagad region organized the Vagad Mazdoor Kisan Sangathan and prepared for the struggle, initially the organization started its work with the struggle for the rights and rights of the people on the land, after which started working on issues like - Community Forest Rights , Displacement, Minimum Wage, Employment Guarantee, Right To Information, Famine Relief, Migration, setting up of Gaon Sabha & recognisation of Gaon Sabha as per in Constitutional Rights, Etc. Vagad Mazdoor Kisan Sangathan is a parallel body with Vagad Mazdoor Kisan Sansthan, which is registered organization under Society Registration Act.

Recent Activities

Reward Distribution for Voluntary Work in Talaiya Village 🛠️

In the Gram Sabha at Talaiya, villagers who contributed through shramdaan (voluntary labor) were rewarded with household items provided by Goonj Sansthan, in collaboration with Vagad Mazdoor Kisan Sansthan. This initiative recognized the villagers’ efforts in local development tasks like road repair and cleanliness, promoting community participation and dignity in labor.

Construction of Check Dam by the People of Doja Gram Panchayat 💧

The villagers of Doja Gram Panchayat came together to construct a check dam to address the issue of water scarcity. This structure will help in recharging groundwater, which in turn will benefit the forest areas, wells, and hand pumps by improving the underground water level.

School Supplies Distribution & Fun Activities in Valota and Hirata 🎒✏️

A special event was held in Valota and Hirata villages where school bags, notebooks, pencils, and pens were distributed to children. The program also featured poetry, games, and competitions, bringing joy and excitement to young students. Supported by Vagad Mazdoor Kisan Sansthan, this initiative aimed to promote education and make learning fun and accessible for rural children.

Road Repair in Talaiya Village 🛠️

With support from Vagad Mazdoor Kisan Sansthan, the road to the cremation ground in Talaiya (Block Dowda, Dungarpur) was repaired, easing travel during rains and reducing animal-related risks. 💪 The work was done through collective village effort, and as appreciation, household items were distributed to participants.
